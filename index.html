02 · Three Blind Spots

Losing ground
without knowing it.

The marketing complexity of running a multi-brand QSR ecosystem has outgrown your current operational tooling. Here are the three places where it is happening right now.

Problem 01 · AI Recommendations
ChatGPT is recommending your competitors

When someone in Lucknow or Indore types "best momos near me" into ChatGPT or Perplexity, a short list comes back. That list shapes purchase decisions before Google is even opened. Right now, there is no tool inside Wow! Momo's stack tracking whether the brand appears in those responses — or whether Bao Bao or any frozen food competitor is named instead.

Problem 02 · Creator Compliance
Your creator programme is scaling faster than your review capacity

#EndOfDebate showed exactly where Wow! Momo's instinct is sharpest — creator-first, participation-driven. But a food brand running creator campaigns across Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, and Kolkata simultaneously is producing hundreds of pieces of content weekly. Manual review is not a process. It is a hope. One unverifiable health claim. One competitor product. One contradictory promo. None of it caught until it's a problem.

Problem 03 · Community Sentiment
Real consumer opinion is forming where you cannot see it

Reddit threads, Discord food communities, Quora questions, and hyperlocal WhatsApp groups are where real Wow! Momo sentiment forms — long before it becomes a review or a media story. Right now, there is a visible gap between strong brand awareness and mixed sentiment around quality consistency and pricing across city tiers. That gap will widen with tier-2 expansion if it is not monitored in near-real time.

The Stakes
The brand that measures first will own it

Momo is absent from, how competitors are scoring, and what content changes would move the brand's position within 30 days. This is the metric that no food brand in India is measuring yet. Not one.
